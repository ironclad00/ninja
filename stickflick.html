Overview

Stick Flick is an Android game concept that I began working on for my Intro to Software Engineering class. The purpose of the class was to become familiarized with agile methodology and version control by working on a software project in 8- or 9-person groups. Right now the app is not publicly available since my group and I are still working on it, but I've discussed how the game works below. All of the displayed artwork for Stick Flick was created by myself unless it is marked with a red asterisk (*).

Stick Flick is a reimagining of the old flash game Defend Your Castle (DyC) by XGen Studios for the Android platform. The objective is to flick incoming enemies into the air so they cannot damage your castle. It is similar to DyC in that it is a tower defence game, you must throw enemies into the air to defend yourself, and you can purchase various powerups that can be used in-game. Differences between the two games are discussed below.

Major Differences

Instead of a side-scrolling perspective that DyC and many similar games use, we wanted to experiment with different perspectives. In Stick Flick, enemies spawn at the castle in the hills and move down the screen towards the player's castle wall at the bottom.

Notice that the player cannot see the bottom of their own wall. If enemies make to the horizon line of your castle, they will run behind the wall, out of view, and damage the castle. Players must use a special ability for killing enemies at the base of the wall called Boiling Oil which is presented in greater detail in the Powerups section.

Background for DyC

DyC progresses using levels. Every time a level is beaten, the player can access a store to buy powerups and then move on to the next, more difficult level. If the player losses or starts a new game, none of the currency from the previous game will be saved.

Instead of a level-based system, Stick Flick is a timed survival game where each game lasts until the player is overwhelmed by increased spawn rates. During each game, players are awarded points based on how long they survive. These points can be spent at the store before/after a game to purchase powerups that will help the player last longer in the next game. A purchased powerup only lasts one game.

Stick Flick also introduces flying enemies. Unlike the standard ground units, flying units spawn behind the mountains, hover above the horizon, and must be drug downwards to be killed.

Enemies

The enemies in Stick Flick are intended to be somewhat comical. Each is referred to as a 'dude' and the final version of their faces is supposed to be the game's logo.

Spring Dude

Powerups

Powerups are used to help players defend their castle for longer periods of time and to reward players for playing the game. They're meant to not only be helpful, but also satisfying and fun to use.

There are two types of powerups that a player can use: passive and active. Passive powerups do not need to be activated and are always running in the background once they are purchased. Active powerups need to be engaged by the user and usually only last for a short duration.


Finger of God

Type: Active

Cost: Moderate

Cooldown: Moderate

Use: Enemies can also be killed by tapping for the next few seconds.

Boiling Oil*

Type: Active

Cost: Free (always available)

Cooldown: Moderate

Use: Kills all enemies that are attacking the base of your castle wall.

Bomb Catapult

Type: Active

Cost: High

Cooldown: Long

Use: Kills all enemies on the screen, including flying ones.

Horn of the Champion*

Type: Active

Cost: High

Cooldown: Moderate

Use: Sends the champion of your castle out into the fray to kill enemies with melee attacks.

Blizzard

Type: Active

Cost: Moderate

Cooldown: Short

Use: Slows every enemy on the screen by 50% for a few seconds.

Archers*

Type: Passive

Cost: Low

Cooldown: N/A

Use: Inflicts constant ranged damage to enemy units. Can kill enemy units without flicking. Can purchase more than once to increase overall DPS.

Mages

Type: Passive

Cost: Low

Cooldown: N/A

Use: Inflicts constant ranged damage to enemy units that is less than the DPS of the archers. Can kill enemy units without flicking. Can purchase more than once to increase overall DPS. Each purchase results in a 0.5% reduced cooldown on every powerup use.

Serfs

Type: Passive

Cost: Low

Cooldown: N/A

Use: Repairs 0.5% of the castle's health every 20 seconds.

Animations

I also created all of the animations for Stick Flick. 2D animations are image files that contain multiple frames of the animated element performing its specific action. I've included examples of some of these animation images here.

From left to right: Friendly archers firing left, demo dude walking forward, boiling oil being poured, priest dude running right, spring dude running forward.

To make animation files like these, I create a 3D model and animation of the element I wanted to animate, such as a stick figure running as seen in this video. Then I split the 3D animation into however many frames I want the 2D animation to have and take screenshots of the 3D model at each split. Every screenshot is then compiled into a single image file.
